My name is Vyom. And I really like movies.

That is, more or less, where this website begins.

**Vyom Vertigo** is a place for me to write about movies, television, books, and stories in general. The **Vyom** part is fairly self-explanatory. The **Vertigo** part comes from my love for Alfred Hitchcock's *Vertigo*. And the tagline is quite simple: **A celebration of movies and stories.**

Because that is ultimately what this is.

Not a place where every movie needs to be declared a masterpiece. Not a place where every bad film needs to be treated as a personal attack on the medium of cinema. And definitely not a place where I pretend to have a definitive answer to everything.

I simply like thinking about stories.

Why a scene works. Why a scene that should work does not. Why one film can be beautifully made and still leave me completely cold. Why another can be objectively terrible and yet give me the best time I have had all week. Why some stories stay with us for years while others disappear from our minds before the credits have even finished rolling.

These are the things I want to write about.

Sometimes that might mean talking about cinematography. Sometimes it might mean talking about writing, performances, editing, sound, music, structure, or the strange decisions that make a movie what it is. Sometimes it might simply mean talking about why I liked something. Or why I hated it. Or, perhaps most dangerously, why I liked something while fully acknowledging that it is not very good.

Because I think that is the interesting part.

A movie does not have to be perfect for you to enjoy it. It does not have to be terrible for you to criticise it. And a story does not need to change your life to be worth talking about. Sometimes, it can simply be a good time. Sometimes, it can make you think. Sometimes, it can make you angry. Sometimes, it can make you stare at the ceiling for twenty minutes after the credits roll.

And sometimes, it can do all of those things at once.

That is what I want to celebrate here: the stories that stay with us, the stories that make us think, and the stories that make us laugh, cry, argue, or question why we spent three hours watching something that was clearly terrible.

So welcome to **Vyom Vertigo**.

A celebration of movies and stories.

I hope you find something here that makes you want to watch something, read something, or think about something you have already watched.

And, ideally, I hope you disagree with me occasionally.

Otherwise, this could get very boring very quickly.
